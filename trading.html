ICC Field Guide
Indication · Correction · Continuation
The Three Steps
1
Indication
Liquidity Pool Penetration — price violently pierces the Previous Day's High or Low (RTH), and a 1-minute Fair Value Gap forms on that break.
Confirm with a Structural Body Close: a candle closing with a solid, full body past the HOD/LOD line. Wicks don't count — a wick is just a sweep, not a break.
2
Correction
Price pulls back into that FVG. If price closes through the far side of the gap, it's invalidated — no trade, walk away.
3
Continuation
Look for a Displacement Candle — one aggressive, full-bodied candle closing back out of the FVG in the Indication direction. This is your entry trigger, not just "price drifting that way."
Check for: body dominance (mostly body, not wick) and a close past the FVG's near edge.
Bonus confirmation: a fresh, smaller FVG left behind by the displacement candle itself.
Invalidated if: the very next candle closes back inside the FVG (a round trip) — that's a failed Continuation. No re-entry on the same FVG; wait for a fresh Indication or abandon the level for the session.
Long (above PDH)

Stop 3 ticks below the FVG bottom.

Short (below PDL)

Stop 3 ticks above the FVG top.

Take profit: fixed 1:2 R while you're building your sample size. Graduate to nearest untaken liquidity once your journal shows which levels actually hold.
Non-Negotiable Rules
Max 3 Trades / Day

Win, lose, or scratch — once you've taken 3, you're done. No "just one more."

Max 2 Losses / Day = Stop

Two losses in a row is your circuit breaker. Close the platform.

This is the rule that directly protects you from revenge trading — it removes your ability to "make it back" the same session.
Position Size Is Locked Before Entry

Set from the risk calculator — never adjusted after a loss. Next trade uses the same or smaller size, never bigger.

The urge to size up after a loss is itself the signal to stop trading, not a signal to act on.
No Revenge Re-Entries

If Correction or Continuation invalidates, that level is dead for the session. No re-entry on the same FVG. No chasing the next candle after a stop-out.

10-Minute Cool-Off After Any Loss

Mandatory walk-away from the charts before you're allowed to look for the next setup. No exceptions.

Pre-Trade Gut Check

Before every entry, ask: "Am I taking this because all 3 ICC steps confirmed, or because I want to be in a trade right now?" If you can't honestly answer the first, don't take it.

Tips & Tricks
Round contract size down, always — never up.
Set your risk $ as a fixed % of account (0.5–1%), not a flat number, so it scales as your account moves.
3-tick stop buffer is the starting default, not 2 — enough to survive normal noise without giving up the "clean" invalidation logic.
Fixed R:R while you're early builds clean, comparable data. Let the journal — not a guess — tell you when to switch to liquidity-based targets.
MNQ is your most fertile instrument for setup frequency. MES is the calmer backup on choppy days. M2K only during RTH — spreads widen outside it.
Volatility is opportunity and decision pressure. The faster the instrument, the more your non-negotiables matter — not less.
Glossary
Liquidity Pool
A cluster of resting stop-loss and breakout orders sitting above/below an obvious level (like PDH/PDL). Price gets drawn toward these pools because there's enough order flow there to fill large positions.
Liquidity Pool Penetration
Price violently piercing the Previous Day's High or Low. This is what kicks off your Indication.
Structural Body Close
A candle closing with a solid, full body past the HOD/LOD line. Wicks don't count — a wick is just a sweep (liquidity grab), not a structural break.
Fair Value Gap (FVG)
A 3-candle imbalance footprint: a gap left behind when price moves so fast that candle 1's range and candle 3's range don't overlap, leaving a visible void. This void tends to act as a magnet — price often returns to "fill" it before continuing.
Unmitigated
An FVG that hasn't been touched/filled yet by price. Once price trades back through it, it's "mitigated."
Displacement Candle
One aggressive, full-bodied candle that closes forcefully out of local structure. This is your Continuation trigger.
Invalidation
The price level where your trade thesis is proven wrong — price closing through the far side of the FVG (Correction fails) or a round-trip back into the FVG right after displacement (Continuation fails).
Point of Control (POC)
The single price level with the most traded volume in a session — the "center of gravity" for that day.
Value Area High / Low (VAH/VAL)
The upper and lower bounds of where roughly 70% of the previous day's volume traded. Your no-trade zone sits between these two.
High-Volume Belly
The dense, thick part of a volume profile where a lot of trading happened. Price tends to chop here rather than trend — this is why it's a no-trade zone.
Tick / Tick Value
The smallest price increment a contract can move, and the dollar amount that increment is worth. Varies by instrument (MNQ, MES, and M2K each have different tick values).
R:R (Risk:Reward)
The ratio between what you're risking (stop distance) and what you're targeting (reward distance). A 1:2 means you're risking $1 to make $2.
