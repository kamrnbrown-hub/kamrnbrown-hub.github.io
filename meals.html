Baked Chickpeas — Shawarma Rice Bowl
Base & Produce
  • Chickpeas — 1 can (13.4 oz), drained & rinsed
  • Basmati rice — ½ cup
  • Red onion — 1
  • Garlic — 1 clove
  • Lemon — 1
  • Grape tomatoes — 4 oz
Toppings & Crunch
  • Pistachios — ½ oz, chopped
  • Cilantro — ¼ oz, chopped
Pantry & Seasoning
  • Shawarma spice blend — 1 tbsp (DIY: cumin + coriander + garlic powder + paprika + pinch allspice/cinnamon)
  • Veggie stock concentrate — 1 tbsp
  • Sour cream — 3 tbsp (swap: vegan sour cream or cashew cream)
  • Butter — 1 tbsp (swap: vegan butter)
  • Hot sauce — 1 tsp
  • Olive oil, salt & pepper
  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Drain and pat chickpeas very dry. Cut onion into ½-inch wedges, mince 2 tbsp of it. Zest & quarter the lemon.
  2. Melt butter in a pot, add minced onion, half the garlic, half the pistachios, half the spice blend — cook 1 min until fragrant. Stir in rice, ¾ cup water, stock concentrate, pinch of salt. Boil, cover, simmer 15–18 min until tender.
  3. Toss onion wedges, tomatoes, and chickpeas with olive oil, remaining spice blend, salt & pepper on a baking sheet. Roast 18–20 min, tossing halfway.
  4. Mix sour cream with remaining garlic, thin with water 1 tsp at a time. Season with salt.
  5. Fluff rice, stir in half the cilantro. Toss roasted veg with lemon zest. Plate rice, top with chickpeas/veg, drizzle garlic sauce + hot sauce, garnish with remaining pistachios & cilantro. Serve with lemon wedges.

Sweet Potato Tacos vegan
  • Sweet potatoes, scrubbed & diced ½-inch
  • Chopped purple onion
  • Chopped mushrooms
  • Grapeseed oil
  • Salt-free multi-spice seasoning
  • Low-salt or salt-free taco seasoning
  • Garlic powder
For Serving
  • Soft or hard taco shells
  • Pico de gallo
  • Sliced avocado
  • Diced purple onion
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Minced jalapeño
  • Fresh lime juice
  1. Boil diced sweet potatoes 5–7 min until just soft. Drain.
  2. Preheat air fryer to 370°F.
  3. Toss sweet potatoes with chopped onion, mushrooms, and a bit of oil.
  4. Add multi-spice seasoning, taco seasoning, and garlic powder — mix gently.
  5. Air fry 15 min, checking halfway to prevent sticking.
  6. Fill taco shells with the mixture, top with pico de gallo, avocado, onion, cilantro, jalapeño, and lime juice.

Vegetable Chow Mein
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup bell peppers (red/green), sliced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional) (swap: vegan oyster/mushroom sauce)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil (optional)
  • 1 tbsp green seasoning (Guyanese-style — thyme, scallion, garlic blend)
  • Salt & black pepper to taste
  • 2 green onions, chopped, for garnish
  1. Boil noodles per package instructions. Drain, rinse with cold water, toss with a little oil. Season with onion, garlic, black pepper, and chow mein seasoning.
  2. Heat oil in a wok/pan over medium-high. Sauté garlic and onion ~30 sec until fragrant.
  3. Add carrots, bell peppers, broccoli, and cabbage. Stir-fry 3–4 min until just tender but still crisp.
  4. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, green seasoning, salt & pepper. Mix well.
  5. Toss in noodles, stir to coat evenly. Cook 2–3 more minutes.
  6. Turn off heat, garnish with scallions. Serve hot.

Vegan Ramen (Sunday Prep + Daily Assembly) vegan
Sunday Broth Prep
  • Bone broth — 6 cups (swap: high-quality vegetable or mushroom broth)
  • Dried shiitake mushrooms — 6–8 whole
  • Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) — 1–2 tsp
  • Low-sodium soy sauce or tamari — 4 tbsp
  • Fresh thyme — 2 large sprigs, tied
  • Fresh ginger — 2-inch piece, sliced
  • Garlic — 4 cloves, smashed
  • Scotch bonnet pepper — 1 tiny sliver (optional)
Daily Assembly
  • Healthy noodles (soba or rice vermicelli) — 1 package
  • Green onion (scallions) — 1 bunch
  • Napa cabbage or radish — 1 small head/bunch, shredded
  • Toasted sesame oil
  • Optional protein: firm tofu (cubed) or avocado (sliced)
  • Cilantro
  • Chili oil
  • Miso or vegetable paste (optional, for aromatics)
Sunday Prep (45–60 min):
  1. Combine broth, shiitake, garlic, ginger, thyme, gochugaru, and scotch bonnet sliver in a saucepan.
  2. Bring to a boil, reduce to low simmer, partially covered, 45–60 min to concentrate flavor.
  3. Turn off heat. Remove and discard thyme, ginger, garlic, and scotch bonnet. Slice softened shiitake to keep in broth if desired.
  4. Stir in soy sauce/tamari. Taste and adjust.
  5. Divide into four ~1.5-cup portions, cool, freeze flat.
Daily Assembly (5 min):
  1. Heat one frozen broth portion with ½ cup water in a saucepan over high heat (1 min).
  2. Boil noodles per package directions (3 min). Optional: sauté mushrooms, green onion, and aromatics/miso in chili oil first.
  3. Thinly slice green onion, shred napa cabbage/radish (2 min).
  4. Drain noodles, place in bowl (30 sec).
  5. Pour hot broth over noodles. Top with green onion, cabbage/radish, cilantro, sesame oil drizzle, and optional protein.

Black Bean Stew vegan
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Green seasoning
  • Spring onion
  • Green and red peppers
  • Black beans
  • Cilantro
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • ¼ tsp whole geera (cumin) seeds
  • ¼ tsp fenugreek (maithe) seeds
  • ½ cup julienned onions
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 pimento peppers/jalapeños, minced
  • 1 tbsp minced chadon beni (culantro)
  • 3 sprigs fine thyme leaves
  • 2½ tbsp curry powder
  • ½ tbsp turmeric powder
  • 1 cup water
  • ¾ lb potatoes, peeled & diced
  • 2 cans chickpeas, drained
  • 3¼ cups water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp geera (cumin) powder
  • 2 tbsp chives, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp celery, finely chopped
  • Salt & black pepper to taste
  1. Heat oil in a pot, toast cumin and fenugreek seeds 30 sec. Add onions, garlic, pimento, chadon beni, thyme sprigs — sauté 2 min.
  2. Add curry and turmeric powders, stir constantly to toast ~2 min.
  3. Add 1 cup water, stir to dissolve spices. Reduce ~4 min into a thick curry paste.
  4. Add diced potatoes and chickpeas, mix to coat, cook 2–3 min to dry any chickpea liquid.
  5. Add 3¼ cups water and salt, scrape the pot bottom. Cover, boil, then reduce to medium — cook 6 min until potatoes are half-cooked.
  6. Uncover, let liquid reduce by half and thicken, about 8–10 more minutes.
  7. Test by pressing a chickpea — should smash easily.
  8. Stir in geera powder, chives, celery, salt & pepper.
  9. Cook further for thicker consistency or serve looser. Don't overcook potatoes. Great with paratha roti.
