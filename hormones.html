Learn the Terms
so you understand exactly what's happening in your body
Estrogen
Your main "builder" hormone. It rises through Follicular phase, peaks right before Ovulation, and is responsible for that boost in energy, mood, skin glow, and confidence you feel mid-cycle.
Progesterone
The "calming" hormone that rises after Ovulation, during Luteal phase. It's soothing in smaller amounts but can bring on bloating, fatigue, and irritability as it rises then drops before your period.
Cortisol
Your main stress hormone. It's meant to spike briefly (like during a workout or a deadline) then drop back down. Chronically high cortisol — from overtraining, undereating, or constant stress — can throw off your entire cycle.
Oxytocin
The "bonding" hormone, released through touch, connection, laughter, and comfort. It directly counters cortisol — this is why a hug or a call with someone you love can genuinely lower stress.
LH Surge (Luteinizing Hormone)
The hormone spike that triggers ovulation itself — the "release" of the egg. It's what's actually being detected by ovulation test strips.
Follicular Phase
The building phase — your body is growing a new egg follicle. It technically starts on day 1 of your period and runs until ovulation, so it overlaps with your period days.
Luteal Phase
The waiting phase after ovulation, when your body prepares for either pregnancy or a period. This is when PMS symptoms typically show up as progesterone falls.
Cycle Syncing
The practice of adjusting your food, exercise, work style, and social life to match your natural hormone rhythm each month, instead of doing the same thing every day regardless of phase.
